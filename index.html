Biography

I was born in Weihai, a small city on the east coast of Shandong Province, China. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) from the School of Information Engineering at China University of Geosciences (Beijing). My undergraduate studies mainly focused on software engineering, and I was trained as a full-stack software engineer. I hold a Master's degree in Cartography and Geographic Information Systems from Peking University. My Master studies focused on several advanced topics in GIS: global georeferencing systems, location-based services, GIS engineering, and Web GIS. I got my Ph.D. degree in Geography (Geographic Information Science & Cartography) from the University of Utah in 2016. My doctoral training concentrated on spatial modeling/simulation, big data, and mobile computing. I did my postdoc in the Center for Systems Integration and Sustainability at Michigan State University, and my postdoctoral training focused on telecoupling and global sustainability.

I am working as an Assistant Professor of GIS in the Department of Geography & Geospatial Sciences at South Dakota State University. I am teaching introductory and upper-level GIS courses. As a certified GIS Professional (GISP), I am dedicated to educating the next generation of GIS professionals and helping them succeed in their career.

I have a long-running research interest in better using geospatial technologies to save life and property in disasters. My current research primarily focuses on wildfire evacuation modeling and simulation in the American west. I will continue this line of research in the next few years to build better wildfire evacuation systems in the U.S. Meanwhile, I also start to work on other topics in hazards, public health, transportation, sustainability, and agriculture.

HashtagHealth

Project Description
HashtagHealth was a project funded by the National Institute of Health (NIH) as a Mentored Research Career Development Award for Dr. Quynh Nguyen at the University of Maryland. This project proposes to design and develop a new resource, HashtagHealth, that addresses both the dearth of neighborhood data and offers novel characterizations of neighborhoods. We will build the data algorithms and infrastructure to harness relatively untapped, cost efficient, and pervasive social media data to develop neighborhood indicators such as food themes, healthiness of food mentions, frequency of exercise/recreation mentions, metabolic intensity of physical activities, and happiness levels.

My Role: GIS Developer/Specialist
I worked as the GIS developer/specialist for this project. My responsibilities include spatial data processing, geovisualization, and web portal development.Specifically, I compiled and merged census tract and zip code zone data for all the states in the U.S. and spatially joined millions of geotagged tweets with the census tracts and zipcode zones they fall within using Python. I mapped out the analysis results (happiness scores, food and exercise mentions) at census tract and zipcode zone levels for three counties and the whole nation. Moreover, I built a web portal that publishes relevant results using WebGIS.

Tools
Python, HTML, JavaScript, CSS, LeafLet, GeoServer,PostgreSQL/PostGIS, ArcGIS

Protective Action Triggers

Project Description
"Protective Action Triggers" is a project funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF), and the principal investigators are Drs. Tom Cova and Phil Dennison in the Department of Geography at the University of Utah. This project aims to improve our understanding of wildfire evacuation timing and warnings by using triggers.

My Role: GIS Modeler/Software Engineer
I worked as a GIS Modeler/Software Engineer for this project. First, I developed a model that uses trigger modeling to stage household-level wildfire evacuation warnings. Second, I constructed a model that can identify prominent geographic features near the wildfire evacuation trigger buffer boundary as trigger points using reverse geocoding and viewshed analysis. Lastly, I coupled wildfire simulation, trigger modeling, and traffic simulation to explore the dynamics behind wildfire evacuation timing.

Tools
C/C++, Python, Java, R, FlamMap, FarSite, ArcGIS

GeoUTA

Project Description
GeoUTA was an android app that is intended to make it more convenient for those who need to catch the bus in Utah. Traditionally, people will use bus schedules to catch a bus. All the Utah Transit Authority (UTA) buses/trains have GPS receivers installed on them and their locations could be retrieved and recorded into the UTA bus tracking database simultaneously.

My Role: Android Developer
I worked as the primary developer for this project.

Tools
Java, Android, Google Map API
